<p><p>The diamond semiconductor substrates market can be categorized into two types: natural and synthetic. Natural diamond substrates are obtained from the earth's crust through mining processes. They possess exceptional thermal conductivity and high electron mobility, making them highly valuable for various electronic applications. On the other hand, synthetic diamond substrates are artificially created using high-pressure, high-temperature (HPHT) or chemical vapor deposition (CVD) methods. These synthetic substrates exhibit similar properties to natural diamonds but can be produced in larger quantities, leading to a more cost-effective option for semiconductor production.</p></p>
