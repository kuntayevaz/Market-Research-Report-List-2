<p><p>Wafer Level Packaging (WLP) is a technology used in the semiconductor industry to package integrated circuits at the wafer level. The main types of WLP are as follows:</p><p>1. 3D TSV WLP: This technology involves stacking multiple layers of chips using through-silicon vias (TSV), enabling high-density interconnects and improving performance.</p><p>2. 2.5D TSV WLP: Similar to 3D TSV WLP, but with a limited number of stacked layers, this technology is used when complete 3D integration is not required.</p><p>3. WLCSP (Wafer Level Chip Scale Packaging): This type involves direct packaging of individual chips on the wafer itself, resulting in smaller form factors and higher performance.</p><p>4. Nano WLP: This emerging technology involves ultra-small feature sizes and allows for extremely compact packaging.</p><p>5. Other types include 2D TSV WLP, which uses through-silicon vias but does not involve stacking, and Compliant WLP, which uses flexible materials to accommodate thermal expansion and improve reliability.</p></p>
